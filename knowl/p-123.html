To do this, we use the handshaking lemma: \(2e=\sum d(v)\text{.}\) Each of the \(n\) carbons has degree 4, so the carbons contribute \(4n\) to the total degree, and each hydrogen of the \(2n+2\) has degree 1 and so only contributes 1 to the sum of degrees. Hence, twice the number of edges is equal to \(4n+(2n+2)=6n+2\text{,}\) and so there are \(3n+1\) edges, as desired.
