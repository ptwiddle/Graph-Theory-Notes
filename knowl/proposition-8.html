Proposition 5.3.9.

Suppose \(\bfG\) has two subgraphs \(\bfG_1\) and \(\bfG_2\text{,}\) so that their union is all of \(\bfG\text{,}\) but their intersection is a single vertex edge \(e\) connecting two vertices \(v\) and \(u\text{,}\) i.e. \(\bfG_1\cup\bfG_2=\bfG\) and \(\bfG_1\cap\bfG_2=\{e\}\text{.}\) Then we have the following relation between their chromatic polynomials:

\begin{equation*} P_{\bfG}(k)=\frac{1}{k(k-1)}P_{\bfG_1}(k)P_{\bfG_2}(k) \end{equation*}
